# Question
For a first-order reaction $A \rightarrow B$, the rate constant is $k = 6.93 \times 10^{-3} \text{ s}^{-1}$. The time required for $90\%$ completion of the reaction is approximately:

# Options
- **A**: $332\text{ s}$
- **B**: $100\text{ s}$
- **C**: $230\text{ s}$
- **D**: $460\text{ s}$

# Explanation
For first-order kinetics: $t = \frac{2.303}{k} \log\left(\frac{[A]_0}{[A]_t}\right)$. For $90\%$ completion, $[A]_t = 0.1[A]_0$. Thus, $t = \frac{2.303}{6.93 \times 10^{-3}} \log(10) = \frac{2.303}{6.93 \times 10^{-3}} \approx 332.3\text{ s}$.
